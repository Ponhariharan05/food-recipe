Ragi Roti

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup ragi flour (finger millet flour)
  • 1/4 cup whole wheat flour (optional, for binding)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds (optional)
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Warm water as required for kneading
  • Ghee or oil for cooking

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine ragi flour, whole wheat flour (if using), finely chopped onions, green chilies, coriander leaves, cumin seeds, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt.
  2. Add warm water little by little to form a smooth dough. The dough should be soft but not sticky. Let the dough rest for about 10-15 minutes.
  3. Divide the dough into small equal-sized balls.
  4. Roll each dough ball into a flat, round disc on a rolling board using a rolling pin. If the dough is sticking, you can dust it with some dry flour.
  5. Heat a tawa or flat pan over medium heat. Once hot, place the rolled-out roti onto the tawa.
  6. Cook for about 1-2 minutes on one side until small bubbles start appearing. Flip the roti and cook the other side for another 1-2 minutes.
  7. Once both sides are cooked, apply ghee or oil on top of the roti and flip it again. Let it cook for a few seconds, then remove it from the tawa.
  8. Serve hot with chutney, curd, or curry of your choice.
