Banana cake

For the Frosting (Optional):

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter (softened)
  • 1 1/2 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 to 2 tbsp milk or heavy cream (adjust for desired consistency)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a 9-inch round or square cake pan or line it with parchment paper.
  2. Mash the ripe bananas until smooth with a fork or potato masher. Set aside.
  3. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt.
  4. In a large bowl, beat the softened butter and sugar together using an electric mixer until light and fluffy (about 2-3 minutes).
  5. Add the eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. Then, add the vanilla extract and mix until combined.
  6. Gradually add the dry ingredients into the wet ingredients, alternating with the buttermilk (or regular milk). Start and end with the dry ingredients. Mix until just combined.
  7. Fold the mashed bananas into the batter gently.
  8. Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan. Bake for 30 to 35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  9. Allow the cake to cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely.

For the Frosting (Optional):

  1. Beat the butter, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract together until smooth and fluffy.
  2. Add milk or heavy cream one tablespoon at a time until you reach the desired consistency.
  3. Frost the cooled banana cake with the frosting, and garnish with sliced bananas or chopped nuts if desired.
