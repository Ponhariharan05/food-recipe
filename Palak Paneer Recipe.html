Palak Paneer Recipe

Ingredients

  • Palak (Spinach): 500g (fresh or frozen)
  • Paneer: 200g (cubed)
  • Onion: 1 large (finely chopped)
  • Tomato: 2 medium (pureed)
  • Ginger-Garlic paste: 1 tbsp
  • Green chili: 1 (slit)
  • Cumin seeds: 1 tsp
  • Turmeric powder: 1/2 tsp
  • Red chili powder: 1 tsp
  • Coriander powder: 1 tsp
  • Garam masala: 1/2 tsp
  • Fresh cream: 2 tbsp (optional)
  • Salt: to taste
  • Oil: 2 tbsp
  • Water: as needed

Instructions

1. Blanch Spinach:
Wash and clean the spinach thoroughly. Boil a pot of water, add the spinach leaves, and blanch them for 2-3 minutes. Drain the spinach and immediately transfer it to a bowl of cold water to retain its green color. Once cool, blend the spinach into a smooth puree and set aside.

2. Prepare the Gravy:
Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds. Let them splutter. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and slit green chili. Sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears. Add pureed tomatoes and cook until the oil separates from the masala.

3. Add Spices:
Add turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Stir well and cook for 2-3 minutes.

4. Cook Palak (Spinach):
Now, add the spinach puree to the pan and mix well. Cook on medium heat for 5-7 minutes. Adjust consistency by adding water if needed.

5. Add Paneer:
Add the cubed paneer to the spinach gravy. Stir gently and cook for another 5 minutes, allowing the paneer to absorb the flavors. Add garam masala and fresh cream (if using), then mix well.

6. Final Touch:
Cook for another 2-3 minutes, then adjust salt and spices according to taste.

7. Serve:
Serve hot with roti, naan, or rice.
